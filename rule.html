Rules

1. Team Composition

  • a. 7 players per team
  • A team must have at least 5 players to start the game, and one of them must be the Center (C).
  • b. Role and court area by position

2. Game Progress

  • a. Game duration
  • b. Timeout: Not allowed during quarters, only during breaks
  • c. Substitution: Decided by the organizer
    1. (1) In domestic games, substitutions are allowed during center pass, halftime, and timeout
    2. (2) Number of substitutions is unlimited unless otherwise stated by the tournament rules

3. Game Method and Rules

  • a. Game Method
    1. (1) Each team has 7 players
    2. (2) Players must remain within their designated areas
    3. (3) The game starts with a center pass after the whistle. Initial positions can vary as long as they don’t enter the center third before the whistle.
    4. (4) Center passes must be received within the center third
    5. (5) Teams alternate center passes after each goal
    6. (6) Dribbling is not allowed
    7. (7) Walking or running with the ball is not allowed (pivoting is permitted)
    8. (8) Defenders must keep a distance of at least 90 cm from the player with the ball
    9. (9) No physical contact is allowed with the ball holder
    10. (10) A pass or shot must be made within 3 seconds
    11. (11) Shots must be taken within the goal circle (GA, GS)
    12. (12) Shooters may take one step while shooting; if the defender is already in position, close defense within 90 cm is allowed
  • b. Fouls and Penalties
    1. (1) Free pass: Given for most minor infractions. The defender must stay 90 cm away.
      1. Footwork – taking too many steps or incorrect foot placement
      2. Three-second rule – holding the ball longer than 3 seconds
      3. Offside – entering areas not allowed for the player's position
      4. Over third – passing the ball over two thirds without touching the center third
    2. (2) Penalty pass: The defender must not interfere until the ball leaves the attacker's hand.
      1. Obstruction – extending arms or defending aggressively within 90 cm
      2. Contact – making physical contact that interferes with play
    3. (3) Warning and suspension: Rough or dangerous play may result in a warning or temporary suspension
